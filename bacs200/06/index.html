Internet of Things (IoT)

The Internet of Things (IoT) is an important topic in technology industry, policy, and engineering circles and has become headline news in both the specialty press and the popular media.

What is the Internet and What is the Internet of Things?

The Internet, commonly called the “Net”, is a system of interconnected computer networks. This network, allows sharing of resources and information. In today’s world, the net is primarily responsible for making it possible to get all kinds of information from anywhere in the world, at our fingertips, no matter where we are, as long as we have “connectivity”.

The Internet of Things (IoT) is the network of physical objects/devices, and digital machines, vehicles, buildings and other items embedded with electronics, software, sensors, and network connectivity that enables these objects to collect and exchange data. They communicate through different methods of use. The methods are the Device-To-Device Communications, Device-To-Cloud Communications, Device-to-Gateway. Applied use of IoT devices are in homes, cities, and cars. It's been project that the total installed base of Internet of Things (IoT) connected devices will amount to 75.44 billion worldwide by 2025
